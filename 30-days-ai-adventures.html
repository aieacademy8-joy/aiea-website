The Explorer Journey

Not just a book.
An adventure journey.

Thirty days. Six destinations. One connected journey.

Young explorers travel with Miss JOY and the AI Explorers crew through a colorful world of discovery. Each five-day adventure introduces a new part of the AI-powered world through illustrated stories, questions, simple challenges, and creative missions.

At the end of each adventure, the Explorer completes a mission, earns a new badge, and records the achievement in the official Explorer Passport — until all six destinations are complete and the child becomes a Future Explorer.

Inside the Learning

Big AI ideas, explained through stories children can understand.

The collection introduces early AI literacy through visual stories, examples, humor, questions, and child-friendly explanations.

Children begin noticing how AI can learn from examples, recognize patterns, make mistakes, and still need people to think, check, guide, and make wise decisions.

This is not about giving children more technology. It is about helping them understand the technology already growing up around them.

Hands-On Missions

The adventure doesn't end on the page.

Explorer Missions transform discovery into action. Young explorers are invited to think, draw, imagine, solve, and create — using their own ideas instead of simply watching technology work.

Mission examples may include:

  • imagine a helpful invention
  • design a future building
  • solve a real-world problem
  • create an idea for a school, library, or community
  • explain how technology might help people
Built for Curious Minds

AI literacy begins with understanding the world around us.

Before children learn to use more AI tools, they need opportunities to notice, question, imagine, and think.

Through the 30-day journey, young explorers begin discovering:

  • where AI may appear in everyday life
  • how technology can help people solve problems
  • why AI is a tool — not the thinker
  • how questions lead to discovery
  • why creativity and imagination still matter
  • how ideas can become helpful solutions
  • why kindness, responsibility, and human choices matter

The future doesn't need children who only know how to use AI. It needs children who know how to think, create, and ask better questions.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is 30 Days of AI Adventures?

It is a connected six-book children's adventure collection that introduces young explorers to the AI-powered world around them through illustrated stories, questions, creative challenges, Explorer Missions, badges, and an Explorer Passport journey.

What age is the collection designed for?

The collection is designed primarily for young explorers around ages 5–8. Younger children may enjoy the journey with more grown-up support, while older curious learners can explore the stories and missions more independently.

Is this a digital download?

No. The planned collection is a physical children's learning experience created for reading, exploring, drawing, completing missions, collecting badges, and using the Explorer Passport.

Does my child need to use an AI tool?

No. The collection is designed to build early AI awareness, curiosity, critical thinking, and creativity. Children explore ideas about AI and the world around them without needing independent access to AI tools.

Is this a coding book?

No. This is not a coding curriculum. It introduces AI literacy through storytelling, examples, imagination, problem-solving, and age-appropriate conversations.

Do children need to complete one adventure every day?

Not at all. “30 Days” creates the adventure structure, but every family can move at its own pace. Complete one adventure a day, explore on weekends, or create your own family rhythm.

What is the Explorer Passport?

The Explorer Passport connects all six books into one journey. Children follow the Adventure Quest Map, record their progress, and celebrate each completed destination as they work toward becoming a Future Explorer.

How do the Explorer Badges work?

Each of the six adventure destinations is connected to an official Explorer Badge. After completing an adventure and its mission, the child unlocks the corresponding badge and adds it to the Explorer journey.

When will it be available?

The Explorer Collection is currently in development for a physical launch. Families can request to be notified when it becomes available.

Can schools, libraries, or after-school programs request bulk information?

Yes. School, library, after-school, homeschool group, and educational program inquiries are welcome. Bulk and partnership availability may differ from individual family orders.

Will international ordering be available?

The initial physical launch is being prepared for the United States. International and bulk ordering options are being explored. Families and educational organizations outside the U.S. are welcome to request availability.
