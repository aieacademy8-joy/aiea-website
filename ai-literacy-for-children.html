Overview

A guide to childhood — not just technology

Artificial Intelligence is changing childhood faster than any previous technology. Children are already learning, creating, searching, and communicating alongside AI — yet most parents, teachers, and schools still ask: Where do we begin? What should children actually know?

Rather than focusing on specific AI tools — which constantly change — the book introduces timeless principles that help children think critically, create responsibly, and develop healthy relationships with artificial intelligence.

The goal is not to make every child an AI engineer. The goal is to help every child become an informed, confident, and responsible citizen in an AI-powered world.

Why This Book Is Different

Most AI books explain technology. This book explains childhood.

Most books teach AI tools. This book teaches AI Literacy. Rather than chasing the latest software, it focuses on the timeless human abilities children will need regardless of how technology changes.

Inside the Book

What you'll learn

Key ideas

  • What AI actually is — and what it is not
  • Why AI Literacy is different from learning AI tools
  • The opportunities and challenges AI creates for children
  • The role of critical thinking in the age of AI
  • Ethics, responsibility, and digital citizenship

Practical guidance

  • Age-appropriate AI education from early childhood through primary school
  • Practical conversations parents can have at home
  • Classroom implementation ideas for teachers
  • School-wide AI Literacy strategies
  • How to prepare children for a future that keeps evolving

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a technical AI book?

No. The book is written for educators, parents, and school leaders without requiring any technical background.

Does the book teach coding?

No. Its focus is AI Literacy rather than programming.

Is this suitable for teachers?

Yes. Teachers will find practical classroom strategies and implementation ideas throughout the book.

Is this suitable for parents?

Absolutely. The book was intentionally written in clear, accessible language for families.

What ages does it cover?

The primary focus is children from early childhood through primary education, with principles that can be adapted beyond those years.
