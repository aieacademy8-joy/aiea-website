Overview

A guide to childhood — not just technology

Artificial Intelligence is already part of childhood. The question is no longer whether children will encounter AI — but whether they will understand how to think, create, and make responsible decisions alongside it.

Parents are asking what children should know. Teachers are being asked to introduce AI without clear guidance. School leaders are making decisions about technology faster than educational frameworks can keep up.

AI Literacy for Children was written for that gap.

Rather than chasing rapidly changing AI tools, this book offers a practical, age-aware way to understand what AI Literacy means for children — from early childhood through the school years.

It will help you ask better questions, make more informed decisions, and begin building an approach to AI that protects what matters most: children's thinking, creativity, judgment, and humanity.

The goal is not to make every child an AI engineer.The goal is to help children become informed, confident, and responsible participants in an AI-powered world.

Why This Book Is Different

Most AI books explain technology. This book explains childhood.

Most books about AI begin with tools.

This book begins with the child.

It explores how Artificial Intelligence is reshaping learning, creativity, information, decision-making, and childhood itself — and what adults responsible for children need to understand now.

Instead of chasing the latest software, AI Literacy for Children focuses on the human capacities children will need regardless of how technology changes.

Because the tools will change. Childhood should not be left to catch up.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a technical AI book?

No. The book is written for educators, parents, and school leaders without requiring any technical background.

Does the book teach coding?

No. Its focus is AI Literacy — timeless principles for thinking, creating, and making responsible decisions with AI — rather than programming or any specific platform or tool.

Is this suitable for teachers?

Yes. Teachers will find practical classroom strategies and implementation ideas throughout the book.

Is this suitable for parents?

Absolutely. The book was intentionally written in clear, accessible language for families.

What ages does it cover?

The primary focus is children from early childhood through primary education, with principles that can be adapted beyond those years.
